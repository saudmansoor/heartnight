Night 10

Ayaan stood at the edge of the park, staring at the rope bridge. It swayed a little every time the wind blew.

"Come on, Ayaan!" his friend Zara called from the other side. "It's fun, I promise!"

Ayaan's tummy felt tight, like it did before a test at school. He wanted to cross. He also really, really didn't want to cross.

He put one hand on the rope. It was rough and cold. He looked down — just once — then quickly looked back up at Zara instead.

"You don't have to look down," Zara said gently. "Just look at me."

One foot on the first plank. It wobbled. He gripped the rope tighter. Another foot. Another plank. The bridge swayed, but it didn't fall — it just moved with him, like it was dancing.

Step by step, plank by plank, Ayaan kept his eyes on Zara's smiling face. And before he knew it, his feet landed on solid ground on the other side.

"I did it," he whispered. Then louder, "I DID IT!"

Zara grinned. "See? Being scared and being brave can happen at the same time."
